Course Description

What is an ethic? Ethics is a set of moral prinicples that govern behavior and decision making.This communication ethics class focuses on ethical issues in the professional workplace. Students learn about different theories and models that are most often applied to ethical issues. There is a focus on ethical issues that are relevant to journalists, public relations, practitioners, advertisers and marketing representatives. The end goal of the course is to understand how to recognize and face ethical issues in upcoming professions as we enter the real world.

My Opinion

I have a hard time understanding ethics with the way Dr. Mackay teaches the class, but she is very passionate about teaching ethics, especially when it comes to journalism. Her slides are very vague and she does not offer a lot more information on the topics in class, which makes studying for the exams difficult.

The class grade was made up of two exams and then a group debate project. The debate topic for my group is explaining why a journalist should publish a story on classified information that they got ahold of. It will be difficult to win the debate considering the rebuttal side will argue us on national security issues. We are using Kant's Categorical Imperative and act utilitarianism to make our point that it is best for the greater good of people.

This is my lowest grade this semester, but that is my own fault. I had a hard time studying for her exams and applying my understanding of the theories.
