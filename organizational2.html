Professional Biography

Before starting her teaching career, Dr. Logan worked in the journalism and media field. She was a freelance writer and photo shoot coordinator for local, national, online and print publications in the Atlanta area. During that team she also worked as an entertainment comlumnist, production assistant and broadcast sales assistant in New York City. After her professional journalism and media experience, she gained professional public relations and corportate communications experience. She was a project coordinator and supervisor, moved up to inside cox magazine editor, to corporate communications manager all within Cox. Then Dr. Logan was promoted to corporate communications manager for the digital side of Manheim, again in Atlanta. Her teaching career started in 2013 with the Virginia Tech Communication Department. She specializes in public relations, organizational communication, corporate communication, rhetorical studies and race. With over 20 years working in communication, she is the perfect professor to be teaching here at Virginia Tech.

Course Description

This course explores the role of communication in complex organizations (e.g., schools, businesses, governments, healthcare systems, non-profits, churches). It focuses on how communication creates and is created by organizations. This course uses theoretical and pragmatic approaches to explore communication networks and flows, message systems, decision-making, and management. At the end of the course, students will be able to recognize and evaluate the various ways organizational communication influences our daily lives.

My Opinion

I really enjoy taking Dr. Logan's organizational communication class. I have learned a lot from it as she focuses it towards real world examples in the workplace, which most of us are entering in the next couple of months. I have enjoyed learning more about leadership styles that work well for me, both to work with and use in my own roles. Dr. Logan is a very understanding professor and will work with you to achieve success. Although the course is online, I feel as if I was able to still build a relationship not only with Dr. Logan, but with the class. She assigned group projects, which at first I thought was crazy wwith it being an online class, but with the technoloigcal advancements these days, there will be continuous online group projects for many careers, and it was important to learn how to work together online.

I would recommend this class to all communication students as it is very informative, and not a lot of work. The class has entailed 10 online assignments, ranging from discussion boards to short papers, two exams and two group projects. It is nice to have a steady calendar knowing each week everything is due on Sunday's. Overall, I have loved this class and will take a lot of my learnings with me to other classes, as well as to my future career.
