Research

His research efforts are devoted primarily to exploring and understanding how professional politicians and citizens publicly address pressing social and cultural issues as these issues are relayed through the mediating lenses of the press. Kuypers is also a pioneer in the area of rhetorical framing analysis. This work has led to important discoveries concerning how original messages of political actors—professionals and citizens alike—are re-framed by the press before being transmitted to the general public. In particular, Kuypers’s methodology allows researchers to investigate how the news media act to shape public awareness, understanding, and evaluations of issues and events in a particular direction.

Course Description

This course explores the role of the mass media (news and entertainment) as sources of social and political influence in contemporary American politics, and on how the mass media and internet sources cover and influence electoral and issue campaigns. Thecourse views communication as functional, and is designed to provide students of all backgrounds and interests with basic communication theory and skills necessary to effectively analyzethe intersection of media and politics.

My Opinion

Media and Politics is one of the more difficult classes I have taken here at Virginia Tech. Politics has never been one of my strong suits and I sometimes find it hard to keep up, especially with other students who worked on Capitol Hill and at CNN this past summer. A lot of my peers really like politics and know what they are talking about, while my Canadian self get confused. I have enjoyed this class and learning about the different ways media plays a role in elections and candidates, and it has challenged me.

The way that Dr. Kuypers teaches his class is a very college level way. He will put basic one word slides up and just talk on the subject for the whole class. It is hard to follow unless you write down literally every word he says in class, or else you will forget and it will be hard to study. There is only two exams and a final project for the overall grade so it is important to really pay attention and do well. He also offers an extra credit opportunity, which is a short paper and totally optional. You can tell he is very passionate about what he is teaching and is truly interested in communication and how it plays a role in all of politics. I have learned a lot from this class and it has sparked my interest in learning more about politics, but I wish sometimes it was a less intimidating class.
